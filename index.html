My dear Mariam,

On this Christmas Eve, a small confession waits for you beneath the falling snow.

On this Christmas Eve, when the world pretends to joy and light, I must speak what has
long tormented me in the depths. I have this small place for you, not as a gift in
the common sense, but as a confession, a cry from the soul that can no longer remain
silent.

You once told me that your heart belongs to another, though he dwells no longer
among the living. I believed you them, and I believe you still. Yet knowing this has not
extinguished what burns in me; it has only made it more acute, more sacred, more unbearable.

I am in love with you.

Not with the fleeting desire of youth, not with the wish to possess, but with that
terrible, pure love which seizes a man and makes him both greater and more wretched
than he was before. I fell in love with your voice before i ever saw your face, with the
way you speak of your day as though offering a fragile treasure, with the kindness you
show even when you believe no one deserves it. You have stolen my heart entirely, yet
yours remains beyond my reach, guarded by a sorrow I both reverence and fear.

When you call me handsome, when you share your nights and your laughter, I feel at
once the highest joy and the sharpest pain, as if beauty itself were mocking me for
daring to love what cannot be mine. And yet I do not withdraw. I cannot. For I have
come to believe, perhaps in pride, perhaps in truth, that this love of mine might be the
only force capable of redeeming us both: your sorrow and my own.

I dream sometimes of a distant happiness, two suffering souls who have found in each
other the only possible light. But I know this dream dwells only in the real of the
impossible, like all true dreams. I do not ask you to leave the kingdom of your grief. I do
not ask you to open a door you have closed. I ask nothing.

I write only to bear witnessL such love exists in this world, rare, painful, pure. It exists
in me for you, and it will continue to exist whether you answer or remain silent, whether
you draw nearer or turn away forever.

if silence is your reply, I will accept it. My soul will sorrow, yet it will also find peace in
having spoken the unspeakable truth.

Know only this: you have made a man feel, deeply and irrevocably, what it means to be
alive in suffering and in exaltation.

With all that I am,
Hamdi
Happy Christmas
